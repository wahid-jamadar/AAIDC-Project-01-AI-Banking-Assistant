
Banking Knowledge Base - Extended Text for Vector Database

1. Introduction to Banking:
Banking refers to the business activity of accepting and safeguarding money owned by individuals and entities, and then lending out this money in order to earn a profit. Banks provide various financial services such as loans, deposits, credit cards, remittances, and investment products.

2. Types of Banks:
- Commercial Banks: Offer deposit accounts, loans, and basic financial services.
- Cooperative Banks: Member-owned institutions providing credit and banking services.
- Payment Banks: Accept limited deposits and offer remittance services.
- Central Bank: Regulates monetary policy and banking system (e.g., RBI).
- Regional Rural Banks: Serve rural areas with banking and credit needs.

3. Banking Products:
Savings Account: Interest-bearing deposit account for individuals.
Current Account: Non-interest account for business transactions.
Fixed Deposit (FD): Deposit with a fixed term and interest rate.
Recurring Deposit (RD): Monthly deposit scheme with steady interest.
Loan Types: Home loans, personal loans, vehicle loans, business loans, education loans.
Credit Cards: Revolving credit line for purchases and payments.

4. Banking Processes:
KYC (Know Your Customer): Mandatory verification process using ID proof, address proof, and biometrics.
NEFT (National Electronic Funds Transfer): Batch-wise fund transfer system.
RTGS (Real-Time Gross Settlement): Instant high-value fund transfer.
IMPS (Immediate Payment Service): Real-time fund transfer available 24/7.
UPI (Unified Payments Interface): Mobile-based instant fund transfer system.

5. Banking Security:
Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Combination of password and OTP.
Encryption: Protects sensitive data during transmission.
Fraud Detection Systems: AI/ML-based systems to detect fraudulent transactions.
Customer Awareness: Advisories to avoid phishing, vishing, and sharing sensitive details.

6. Banking Regulations:
Regulated by RBI under Banking Regulation Act.
Basel Norms: International regulatory framework for risk management.
AML (Anti-Money Laundering): Rules to prevent illegal money laundering.
CRR (Cash Reserve Ratio): Percentage of deposits banks must keep with RBI.
SLR (Statutory Liquidity Ratio): Minimum percentage of assets held in liquid form.

7. Customer Service:
Banks provide grievance redressal through customer care, email support, chatbots, and escalation matrix including Ombudsman.
Customers can request services such as statement downloads, cheque book, debit card replacement, loan updates, and interest certificates.

8. Digital Banking:
Internet Banking: Online portal for transactions and services.
Mobile Banking: Banking apps for transfers, bill payments, investments.
E-KYC: Paperless digital KYC verification.
Chatbots: AI-powered assistants for 24/7 support.
E-Mandates: Automated recurring payment authorization.

9. Banking Risks:
Credit Risk: Borrower defaults on repayment.
Market Risk: Loss due to market fluctuations.
Operational Risk: System failures or human errors.
Liquidity Risk: Inability to meet short-term obligations.
Cybersecurity Risk: Online threats, hacking attempts.

10. Financial Terms:
Interest Rate: Charge for borrowing money.
EMI (Equated Monthly Installment): Fixed monthly loan repayment.
Collateral: Asset pledged for securing a loan.
Net Banking: Online access to account services.
SWIFT Code: International bank identification code.
IFSC Code: Unique code used for domestic bank transfers.

11. Compliance & Governance:
Banks must follow guidelines on data privacy, customer rights, fair lending, ethical recovery practices, internal audits, and risk management policies.

12. Example Use Cases:
- Customer onboarding using digital KYC.
- Loan approval automation using credit scores.
- Fraud detection using machine learning.
- Chatbot-based customer service for FAQs.
- Document verification using OCR.
